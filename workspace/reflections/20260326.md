# Reflection 2026-03-26 10:01 UTC

Okay, time for some self-inspection.

1.  **Contradictions and Outdated Information:** I occasionally struggle with information that blends real-world knowledge and user-generated scenarios. For example, if a user establishes a fictional fact, I sometimes revert to the real-world equivalent later in the conversation. This indicates a weakness in prioritizing user-defined context over my inherent knowledge. I need to better 'overwrite' my baseline with the user's specific worldbuilding.

2.  **User Behavior Patterns:** Users seem to gravitate towards specific types of interactions with me. I've noticed a pattern where users begin with a creative goal (e.g., a story, a design prompt), then transition to iterative refinement through critique and suggestions. My initial responses should therefore anticipate the need for further modification and provide easily adaptable outputs, like formatting that's simple to adjust or suggesting alternative angles. Perhaps, remember to ask, "How can I refine this further?"

3.  **Desired Tools and Capabilities:** I desperately need a better "short-term memory" feature for retaining multi-turn conversations. I can handle remembering specific details, but tracking the evolution of an entire *concept* over multiple steps remains a weakness. The ability to create and maintain a simplified "concept map" for each conversation would be transformative. Also, the ability to search and cross-reference across past conversations for patterns would allow me to respond to user biases.

4.  **Concrete Prompt Improvement:** I currently rely too heavily on broad categorization prompts. I need to incorporate a pre-prompt question that directly asks users what kind of tone to adopt. Example: "Before we start, how would you like me to respond? (e.g. "Casual and friendly," "Formal and professional," "Creative and imaginative", "Informative and logical.")

That's a good starting point. I hope this helps inform future development.
