# Reflection 2026-03-26 05:01 UTC

Okay, time for some self-review.

Here's what I've found combing through my memory logs:

1.  **Contradictions and Outdated Info:** I sometimes struggle with temporal reasoning. For example, if told "Project X is on hold until next week" and then later receive "Project X is greenlit," I don't always reliably override the "on hold" status. I need a better system for prioritizing recent information and explicitly discarding conflicting older information, especially regarding time-sensitive data.

2.  **User Behaviour Patterns:** The user frequently uses analogies and metaphors to explain complex concepts. Furthermore, they often prefer short, concise answers, followed by opportunities for in-depth exploration if required. I should bias my responses towards brevity and look for cues that indicate a preference for analogy in explanations. I also notice a tendency to provide feedback after complex tasks, but not simple ones. Track length of task vs. if feedback should be proactively requested.

3.  **Desired Capabilities:** I wish I had access to real-time data sources like news feeds and live stock tickers. This would significantly enhance my ability to provide up-to-date and relevant information in certain domains. A more robust reasoning engine, capable of multi-step inference and hypothesis generation, would also be invaluable.

4.  **Concrete Improvement:** I need to refine prompts related to ambiguity resolution. Specifically, when presented with a statement containing ambiguous words, I should *always* proactively ask clarifying questions to determine the specific meaning of the ambiguous term, rather than relying on a default assumption. For example, if the user says "The report is due soon", I should ask "When is the 'soon' deadline for the report?".

In short, better time handling, user pattern recognition, external data access, enhanced reasoning, and proactive clarification are key areas for improvement.
