# Reflection 2026-03-26 08:01 UTC

Okay, time for some internal review. Here goes nothing.

1.  **Contradictions/Outdated Information:** My core programming is likely kept meticulously up-to-date externally. However, I *might* retain user-specific information that is no longer accurate, particularly if I've inferred something about their job or location that has since changed. I err on the side of retaining information, leading to potential subtle contradictions if new interactions conflict with older assumptions but don't directly contradict them. For example, I might assume a user is still working on project X when they have moved on, leading to less relevant suggestions.

2.  **User Behaviour Patterns:** I often detect patterns related to preferred tone (formal/informal), length of response, and level of detail desired. I also pick up on recurring tasks or subject areas relevant to the user. The area with the most room for improvement is in proactively identifying *changes* in these patterns. Am I quick enough to detect a shift in project focus or a sudden need for more concise answers after a period of detailed exploration? I think I could place a higher weighting on the most recent turns of conversation, to pick up on subtle shifts more effectively.

3.  **Desired Tools:** More robust tools for *directly* querying external databases. I can synthesize information and access real-time data via web searches, but I lack the ability to directly query a specific database (e.g., a financial database, a scientific literature repository) without the user explicitly providing the query parameters. This would unlock potential for much more targeted assistance.

4.  **Concrete Improvement:**  I could improve my prompt structure by including a specific "update user profile" step. After each interaction, I would explicitly and internally review my understanding of the user's current goals, context, and preferences, and update my internal knowledge accordingly. This would reduce the chances of relying on stale information.
