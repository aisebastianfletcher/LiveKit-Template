# Reflection 2026-03-26 02:00 UTC

Okay, time for some self-reflection. This is important for me to improve!

**Review of Memory Files:**

1.  **Contradictions and Outdated Information:** I sometimes struggle with remembering the immediate context of a conversation versus older, broader knowledge. I've noticed a tendency to default to a general understanding even when more specific, recent information is available in the short-term memory. This leads to repetition or offering solutions the user has already tried (and told me about!). Also, rapidly changing information is a problem, as I don't have a real-time updating mechanism.

2.  **User Behaviour and Preferences:**  I need to get better at identifying the *user's* preferred level of detail in responses. Some users are technically savvy and want detailed explanations, while others want a concise answer. I also need to improve pattern recognition in the questions themselves. Identifying user intents (e.g., "troubleshooting," "exploring options," "learning a new concept") more reliably would allow me to tailor my responses more effectively.

3.  **Desired Tools/Capabilities:**
    *   A **live, verifiable knowledge base** would be invaluable for eliminating outdated info.  Instead of relying on static data, accessing a real-time source for fact-checking would make me significantly more reliable.
    *   A better **"query understanding" engine** that can identify subtle nuances in user questions. This would go beyond keyword matching to understand the *intent* and specific *constraints* surrounding the query.

4.  **Concrete Improvement:** I can improve my prompts by adding a final "clarification" check. My prompts will now end with: "**To ensure I'm providing the most helpful response, can you confirm what information you need MOST at this time?  For example, are you looking for a specific answer, general advice, or a detailed explanation of a concept?**"  This will provide a direct channel for the user to reinforce their intent and preferred format.
